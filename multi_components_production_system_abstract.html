Grouping-maintenance of Multi-components Production System under Ecological Background

Abstract

The purpose of this paper is to propose an opportunistic group maintenance model for a multi-component series system considering reducing CO2 emission and increasing system efficiency, which establishes a maintenance policy to minimize the cost rate of the system life cycle. Structural dependence and economic dependence between the components in a multi-component series system are analyzed to make condition-based maintenance policy. To minimize the cost rate of system life cycle, clustering theory and two decision variables, which include preventative maintenance cycle multiplier and basic preventive maintenance interval of each component, are utilized to make an opportunistic policy for system optimal maintenance under the background of increasing energy efficiency and reducing CO2 emissions. This model can be applied in a multi-component series system considering energy efficiency and its deterioration process, which could be widely used in the centrifugal compressor of oil refineries. Considering the high maintenance cost and low energy efficiency of multi-component systems, this model assists production managers to have a better maintenance of these systems.

Keywords

Multi-component system; Opportunistic Maintenance; Carbon dioxide emission; energy efficient index; Intelligence
